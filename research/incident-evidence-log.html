Persistent VPN Certificate Surviving App Uninstallation Critical
Date: 30 May 2026 · 16:54 Device: Xiaomi 11T Pro Apps involved: Proton VPN (Play Store), 1984 VPN (F-Droid) Certificate: identity-cert-keypair-2
Both Proton VPN and 1984 VPN were uninstalled cleanly via standard uninstallation. However, certificate identity-cert-keypair-2 (containing 1 user key + 1 user certificate, installed for VPN and apps) remained on the device after both apps were removed. The "-2" suffix indicates a prior keypair existed, meaning the certificate had been rotated or replaced at least once previously. Internet connectivity failed specifically on the home WiFi network while remaining functional on all other networks — consistent with a targeted MITM intercept on the home network that failed or conflicted, breaking home connectivity while leaving other connections intact. A full network stack reset was required to clear the certificate. After a subsequent reboot, the uninstalled VPN app reappeared without user action.
Forensic Significance — Certificate Persistence
Standard VPN app certificates are removed with the app. A certificate surviving uninstallation indicates it was installed at system or privileged level — not by the VPN app itself, but by a separate privileged process using the VPN app as cover.
Forensic Significance — App Resurrection After Reboot
CRITICAL: A manually uninstalled app reappearing after reboot cannot be explained by any user-level process. This behaviour is only possible if a privileged system component — in /system/priv-app, the DSU layer, or an AVF virtual machine — is reinstalling the app silently during the boot sequence. This is direct evidence of a persistent privileged process actively maintaining the VPN infrastructure independent of user actions.
Resolution
Full network stack reset required. Certificate cleared after reset. Internet connectivity restored.
Internet Connectivity Broken — Network Settings Altered High
Date: ~28–31 May 2026 Device: Xiaomi 11T Pro
Internet connectivity stopped working. Investigation revealed network settings had been altered without user action. The persistent VPN certificate (Incident 2) was identified as related. Connectivity was restored only after a full network stack reset.
Forensic Significance
Spontaneous network settings alteration without user action is consistent with a privileged background process modifying routing or proxy configuration. Combined with the persistent certificate, this suggests an attempt to force-route traffic through a controlled channel that failed due to a configuration conflict, breaking connectivity in the process.
